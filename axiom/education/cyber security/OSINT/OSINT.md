**OSINT** stands for **Open Source Intelligence**.

In short, it is the practice of legally and publicly collecting, analyzing, and using data from open sources to produce actionable intelligence.


This does not mean open-source software. It means any information that is publicly accessible to anyone. No hacking, no stealing, and no private databases required.
It’s is not gathering raw data. It’s about connecting the dots, analyzing the pieces, and turning them into meaningful knowledge.

### Where does OSINT data come from?

It comes from Social Media, liek Public profiles, posts, photos, and check-ins.
It searches for hidden PDFs, leaked files and archived pages.
Through Government archives, domain registration, company records, and real estate data.
Also Geospatial Data gets analyzed, like Satellite imagery (Google Earth) and street-level photos to pinpoint exact locations (GEOINT).
